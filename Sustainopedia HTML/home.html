1. What is Sustainable Development?

Sustainable development is the organizing principle for meeting human development goals while at the same time sustaining the ability of natural systems to provide the natural resources and ecosystem services upon which the economy and society depends. The desirable end result is a state of society where living conditions and resource use continue to meet human needs without undermining the integrity and stability of the natural systems.

2. Why is Sustainable Development important?

Sustainable development is important because our global future depends on it. It's no secret that people are living longer and that the global population is on the rise. In fact, the United Nations projects that there will be more that 10 billion people living on the Earth by the year 2100. This explosion in population is perhaps one of the greatest reasons why sustainable development is so important.

3. Steps undertaken by Indian Government to achieve Sustainability-

i) Poverty Eradication

ii) Changing Unsustainable Patterns of Consumption

iii) Empowerment of Green Energy Generation and Production

iv) Building Sustainable housing

v) Empowering usage of Green Fuels
